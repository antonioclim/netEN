## Slide 3 - Where sockets fit
- Application layer API exposed by the OS
- Mapping: application data -> TCP segment or UDP datagram -> IP packet -> link frame

## Slide 4 - TCP quick recap
- Connection-oriented
- Handshake and teardown
- Flow control and congestion control (high level)

## Slide 5 - UDP quick recap
- Connectionless
- No delivery guarantees
- Low overhead and useful for simple request/response patterns

## Slide 6 - TCP server workflow
- socket() -> setsockopt() -> bind() -> listen() -> accept() -> recv()/send() -> close()
- Diagram: vertical flow of calls

## Slide 7 - Iterative vs concurrent servers
- Iterative: one client at a time
- Concurrent: threads or processes
- Trade-offs: simplicity, latency under load and shared state

## Slide 8 - Exercise 1 overview
- Script: ex_2_01_tcp.py
